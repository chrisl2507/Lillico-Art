About

The Artist Behind the Work

Lillico Art is the studio of a UK-based artist specialising in hand-drawn portraiture. Working primarily in charcoal and graphite, each piece is crafted to capture not just a likeness, but a feeling — the quiet tension in a glance, the weight of a resting hand, the light that falls across a face.

Drawing has always been the most honest way I know to look at someone. There's something about the slow, deliberate process of building a portrait by hand — mark by mark — that a photograph simply cannot replicate.

My work sits at the intersection of fine art and portraiture. I draw from life and from reference, and I'm particularly drawn to natural, unposed moments. The kind of image you'd frame not because it's perfect, but because it's true.

"Every face tells a story. My job is to listen with a pencil."

Process

Materials & Method

Charcoal

The primary medium. Willow and compressed charcoal on heavyweight cartridge paper. Deep blacks, soft gradients, and a rawness that suits portraiture perfectly.

Graphite

For finer detail work. Mechanical and clutch pencils on smooth Bristol board. Precision drawing for hands, eyes, and intricate studies.

Mixed Media

Charcoal and chalk on toned paper, or ink washes with graphite. Used when a piece calls for warmth, texture, or a sense of the paper as part of the work.
